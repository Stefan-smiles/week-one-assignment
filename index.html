A Brief Decsription of a Demon

A demon is a spiritual, noncorporeal being that is believed to be malevolent and seek to harm humans. Demons are often depicted in religious iconography as hybrid creatures or caricatures of opposing religions. In the Christian tradition, demons are angels who fell from grace.


A demon can also refer to a source of evil, harm, distress, or ruin. For example, someone might refer to drug or alcohol addiction as a demon. The word “demon” comes from the Greek word daimōn, which can refer to a variety of beings, not just evil ones.

Classifications of Demons

There are many types of demons, including:

  • Theological demons: These include Satan, Samael, Shedim, and others.
  • Demons associated with vices: These include Lucifer (pride), Mammon (greed), Asmodeus (lust), Leviathan (envy), Beelzebub (gluttony), and Satan (wrath).
  • Demons in folklore: These include the succubus, a female demon that appears in dreams to seduce men, and the incubus, the male equivalent.
  • Demons in Buddhism: These include Mara, an arch tempter, and his daughters Rati, Raga, and Tanha.
  • Demons in Chinese religions: These include the guei-shen, which are nature demons.
  • Jinn: These are fire-composed animals that can appear in many forms, including the Shaitan, a malevolent jinn, and the Ifrit, a powerful and cunning jinn.
  • Asuras and Rakshasas: These are pious and highly enlightened beings that are not inherently evil.
Demons are often categorized as benevolent, malevolent, ambivalent, or neutral beings
